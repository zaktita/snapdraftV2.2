**TL;DR:** Fixing one post should not mean redoing forty. SnapDraft regenerates a single spreadsheet row against the same Brand DNA so iteration stays cheap.

Batches fail in the real world when fixing one post means touching forty others. Row-level regeneration exists so iteration stays cheap.

## Why whole-batch redo is expensive

Prompt-only tools often force you to re-run a conversation or a mega-prompt when a single headline changes. You lose time and you invite new drift across assets that were already approved.

## Isolate the change

In a spreadsheet-native workflow, row 37 is a unit:

- Same Brand DNA
- Same format column
- Updated title or description only

Regenerate that row. Leave the rest of the week alone.

## When to regenerate vs edit in Canvas

- **Regenerate** when the concept is wrong (wrong subject, wrong mood relative to the brief).
- **Canvas** when the concept is right but the last mile is wrong (typo, logo swap, crop, distraction erase).

That split keeps [content calendar to visuals](/glossary/content-calendar-to-visuals) moving without perfectionism paralysis.

## Ops tip for agencies

Train account managers on Canvas for light feedback. Escalate to regenerate only when the brief itself changed. That is how you protect design craft for work that actually needs it. See [SnapDraft for agencies](/use-cases/agencies).
