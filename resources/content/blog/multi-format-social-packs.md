**TL;DR:** Cropping after the fact breaks on-brand packs. Put format in the SnapDraft CSV so each row generates for feed, story, or banner correctly.

Cropping after the fact is where on-brand batches quietly die. Faces get clipped. Logos slide off. Story frames look like afterthoughts.

## Put format in the brief

Treat aspect ratio as a first-class column next to title and description. Each row declares whether it is feed, story, or banner **before** generation.

That is the core of the [multi-format export](/templates/multi-format-export) workflow template.

## Why native generation beats resize

Resizing a square into a story is not the same as generating for portrait composition. Brand DNA still applies, but framing decisions change with the canvas. Generate for the format you will actually publish.

## Delivery hygiene

When you download:

- Keep naming consistent with the sheet (row id or date + title)
- Group by format if your scheduler wants separate folders
- Spot-check a sample of each ratio in Canvas if framing feels tight

## Who feels this pain most

Social managers scheduling mixed grids and story series. See [SnapDraft for social media managers](/use-cases/social-media-managers). Agencies handing packs to clients who post across placements the same week also hit this hard.
