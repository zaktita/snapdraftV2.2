**TL;DR:** Weekly publishing fails when production is a queue. With SnapDraft it becomes a loop: plan in CSV, generate against Brand DNA, polish in Canvas, export to your scheduler.

Weekly publishing fails when production is a queue. It works when production is a loop you control.

## Monday: plan in the sheet

Keep the calendar where it already lives. Require three columns at minimum:

1. **Title** - hook or headline on the visual
2. **Description** - what the image must communicate
3. **Format** - square, portrait, or landscape

That schema is enough for [CSV batch generation](/glossary/csv-batch-generation).

## Tuesday: generate against Brand DNA

Upload the sheet to a SnapDraft project with Brand DNA already set. Review the batch as a set. Not as isolated prompts. You are checking coherence first, cleverness second.

## Wednesday: regenerate and Canvas

Weak rows get a regenerate with the same inputs, or a focused Canvas pass (swap, erase, expand, upscale). Do not reopen a design ticket for last-mile copy and object tweaks.

## Thursday and Friday: export and schedule

Download the pack sized correctly, then drop into Later, native schedulers, or client delivery folders. SnapDraft sits upstream of scheduling. See how that differs from [Later alternatives](/alternatives/later).

## Who this loop serves

- [Social media managers](/use-cases/social-media-managers) closing the designer wait
- [Freelancers](/use-cases/freelancers) delivering same-day batches
- [Agencies](/use-cases/agencies) keeping multi-brand calendars full

Start with next week's real calendar. Not a demo dataset.
